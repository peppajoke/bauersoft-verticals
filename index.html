BauerSoft: What Can You Actually Ship

5 opportunities filtered through real constraints. Not "big markets that exist" but "things you can build, sell, and collect revenue on in 90 days or less."

Hard Constraints Applied

  • Solo founder + day job
  • No sales team
  • No domain expertise required
  • JS/TS/Postgres stack
  • AI (Claude/GPT) is the product
  • Revenue in <90 days
  • No regulatory compliance burden
  • $0 upfront capital
Key Insight: Don't Pick a Vertical. Pick a Workflow.

The dataset has 318 business types. 176 of them are owner-operator accessible with low compliance. The opportunity isn't "build software for dentists." It's "solve one painful workflow that 500k+ businesses share, and let AI be the moat."

Methodology

Source: 318 US business types scored across 40 dimensions (May 2026). Filtered for: owner-operator ratio >=4, decision-maker accessibility >=4, regulatory compliance <=3, sales cycle <=1 month, AI advantage >=3. Then grouped by shared workflow pain, not vertical. Revenue estimates are conservative.

Previous analysis (HVAC deep-dive, CrewOne platform concept) explored the trades vertical. This analysis asks a different question: what's the smallest thing you can ship that generates revenue fastest?
